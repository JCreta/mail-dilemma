Local Woman, Former Steelworker, About to “Go Full New York” on USPS After Yet Another Lost Package

CHARLOTTE, NC – USPS Has Officially Moved to the Top of Gina Thompson's "Problems to Solve" List

Seventy-one-year-old Gina Thompson has spent a lifetime working hard, first in the steel mills and now from the comfort of her home, where she logs hours for a national beverage company. But no amount of grit, determination, or old-fashioned elbow grease can protect her from the *absolute clown show* that is the United States Postal Service.

“Back in *my day*, people actually gave a damn about their jobs,” Gina fumed, gripping a crumpled tracking receipt like it was evidence in a federal RICO case. “You think I could’ve just *forgotten* to weld a beam and told my boss, ‘Oopsie, I *attempted* to put it there’? No, I’d have been tossed into the furnace.”

Gina, a proud product of the *Tri-State area*, has never been one to take incompetence lightly. Raised in a world where “if you didn’t hustle, you didn’t eat,” she has zero patience for what she calls “the USPS participation trophy brigade.”

“They expect me to believe they knocked, rang, and just *coincidentally* I didn’t hear them? Yeah, okay, genius,” she scoffed. “I *work from home*. I was sitting *right here*, processing invoices, drinking my Diet Coke, and listening to my dogs lose their damn minds over a squirrel 50 feet away. You think I wouldn’t hear some government lackey fumbling around my porch? My *Ring camera* says otherwise, bucko.”

But it’s not just the lying that has Gina steaming—it’s the sheer *stupidity*.

“I worked in a *steel mill*, sweetheart. I lifted things that would make these little mailroom Mickeys cry for their mommy. And yet, somehow, I still managed to *do my job correctly*. These people can’t even put a box on a porch without turning it into an unsolved mystery. I swear, it’s like dealing with a bunch of *mental midgets*.”

Now, Gina is ready to handle things the way she was taught: with a direct approach and absolutely no regard for anyone’s feelings.

“Oh, I’m *going down there*,” she declared, cracking her knuckles like a mob enforcer about to collect a debt. “And I’m bringing *Tri-State energy* with me. I may live in North Carolina, but don’t get it twisted—I will go *full New York* on their asses. I don’t care if I have to burn my entire afternoon standing at that counter, glaring at some clueless government lifer who thinks ‘customer service’ means *breathing in my direction*—I *will* get my damn package.”

She took a deep breath, adjusting her ‘Don’t Tread on Me’ coffee mug.

“I’m gonna make *such a scene*, they’ll be naming a postal reform bill after me.”

As of press time, Gina had already started compiling a list of grievances dating back to the Bush administration and had printed out USPS’s official policies, which she intended to *slam* onto the counter for dramatic effect.

The postal carrier assigned to Gina’s route has reportedly filed for witness protection.
